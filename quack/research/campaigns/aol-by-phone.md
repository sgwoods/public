# AOL by Phone and post-acquisition product path

Focused follow-up campaign for the Quack to AOL by Phone transition and the product layer that mattered after the August 31, 2000 AOL acquisition.

## Why this campaign exists

- Quack is no longer just an acquisition story; the October 25, 2000 `AOL Formalizes 'Anywhere' Strategy` report ties Quack directly to the launched `AOL by Phone` service and names SpeechWorks as the voice-recognition layer.
- The current archive has good acquisition coverage but only one direct product-transition source, so the post-acquisition product story is still thinner than the exit story.
- First-party or archived AOL-owned web pages would materially strengthen this lane because they would show how AOL described the service once Quack technology moved inside the AOL Anywhere product family.

## Best current sources

- `src-quack-2000-aol-formalizes-anywhere-strategy`: [InternetNews, October 25, 2000](https://www.internetnews.com/it-management/aol-formalizes-anywhere-strategy). Strong direct product-transition source linking `AOL by Phone`, Quack, and SpeechWorks.
- `src-quack-2000-another-feather-in-aols-cap`: [InternetNews, August 31, 2000](https://www.internetnews.com/it-management/another-feather-in-aols-cap). Strong acquisition anchor for the pre-launch to post-launch transition.
- `America Online Launches AOL 6.0`: [Streaming Media, October 25, 2000](https://www.streamingmedia.com/Articles/News/Online-Video-News/America-Online-Launches-AOL-6.0-63266.aspx). Useful supporting launch coverage for the same product moment.

## Next capture targets

- Look for archived AOL-owned pages around `phone.aol.com`, `anywhere.aol.com`, or AOL 6.0 launch pages from October 25, 2000 through early 2001.
