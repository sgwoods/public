# Quack first-party capture targets

This note tracks first-party or AOL-owned web targets that would materially improve the Quack artifact lane if preserved.

## Why these matter

The Quack archive already has strong contemporaneous press, but first-party pages would make the company and post-acquisition product story much more concrete. In particular, the October 25, 2000 `AOL Formalizes 'Anywhere' Strategy` article says `AOL by Phone` launched that day and explicitly describes it as a voice portal built on Quack technology with SpeechWorks voice recognition.

## Priority targets

- `quack.com` homepage and any product or help pages from 1999 to 2000
- AOL-owned `AOL by Phone` pages from October 25, 2000 onward
- AOL Anywhere launch pages that describe telephone access as part of the new platform
- Any surviving Quack or AOL screenshots, onboarding pages, or FAQ/help pages that show the actual feature surface

## Practical target domains

- `quack.com`
- `aol.com`
- `phone.aol.com`
- `anywhere.aol.com`
