READ THIS!!  EE150 Academic Guidelines

Cheating or plagarism is a serious academic offense.  It can lead very
quickly to a failed grade and permanent record on your transcript,
or even expulsion from University.

Acceptable Help:

Discussing approaches to problems is acceptable.  Seeking tutor
or student assistance with concepts is acceptable.  I have office
hours to help with your problems - I also am open to making an
appointment to discuss anything at all. 

Not-Acceptable Help:

Having a tutor debug your code is not acceptable.  Having another
student debug your code is not acceptable.  Sharing code is not
acceptable in any form.  Showing your solutions to others in EE150 is not
acceptable.  

During the course of reviewing solutions to Assignment (mini) 3 it has
become obvious that sharing of code (exact code) has already become 
a problem.  As assignments increase in complexity and difficulty such
cheating will become even more obvious.  After the midterm I will
have an excellent idea of each students' ability and fudged
assignments will stand out.

In a word - don't cheat - I will *not* be in any way lenient with
anyone caught cheating or sharing solutions to assignments.  It is
unfair to those students who do not cheat to tolerate any such behaviour.
