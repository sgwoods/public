Hello Fall. This shoulder season expect some superb courier deals. Here's a start: Now Voyager(NY) offers further discounts for its flights for tickets purchased with cash vs. credit. Though I encourage buying tickets with plastic for maximum recourse if anything should go wrong, if you're really in for the savings, use greenbacks. NV is again selling discounted regular fares on Pakistan Int'l Air to Frankfurt/Amsterdam for $485 r/t.

If you've never flown with the Pakistan's national carrier, the service out of New York is excellent and the cuisine fresh and spicy; the return flight is harried as the plane originates in Karachi and everyone and their kids are packed in for the lengthy haul . . . both DTI's new NY and Miami tel. #s. are simply (800) FLY4DTI. Ask them this month about Milan for $299 r/t! They're also offering NY-Jo'burg direct on South African Airways for $899 r/t - a deal.

DTI is also selling discounted confirmed seats to/from Denver, Seattle, and Anchorage. I've added Halbart's Washington DC-London courier flight. Like the Boston-London, this month for $328 r/t. Everything from Hong Kong and England has been completely updated - know that most offices abroad can book tickets by telephone from the US before you arrive. And once again Iceland Air is again selling $348+ r/t NY-Luxembourg/$398+ r/t to London, (800) 223-5500 - real cheapo to Europe!

Tid Bits: Take the F Train . . . The Carey buses in and out of NY's airports and taxis are a major rip off. Do as the rest of the world does - take the local bus! Really? Here's how: F Train to Roosevelt Avenue and the Q33 bus to La Guardia. To JFK, take the F Train to Kew Gardens and the Q10 bus. $2 vs. $13-$25!? (from the NYT Op Ed sec., Jul 28). Mea Culpa - Transitions' Annual Resource Guide is $6.95, not $5 - oops. Still worth every penny! Marazul's daily charters Miami-Havana have been curtailed due to Pres. Clinton's recent tightening of the Cuban embargo. Fear not - travel to Cuba through via Mexico or Canada.

Buckets: When I teach travel here in the Boston area, folks always ask about options other than courier flights for rock-bottom prices. Among other things I suggest they peruse the fine print discount flight listings in the back of the travel section of the New York or LA Times. Bucket shops can give outstanding savings, and sometimes outstanding headaches due to the unreliability of many offices and their fly-by-night attitudes. Here's a brief listing of several buckets that have some reputation for decent service: RA Travel (800) 788-0777; Up & Away Travel (800) 275-8001; TFI (800) 745-8000; Flytime (212) 760-3737; and try Council Charter (800) 800-8222. And with a specialty in African flights: Lan-Si Aire (212) 889-6305; Spector (800) 879-2374 or (617) 338-0111. Most of these are in NY, but buckets can schedule flights from most US detinations. -> Some questions to always ask before purchase: Is this ticket refundable? Is the ticket transferable or able to be altered at extra cost? Are taxes included? Is there a service charge/fee? Is it more on weekends? Can I claim frequent flyer miles (if no, claim them anyway at the gate or by telephone). Like a used car dealer, buckets are quick to sell you something and don't appear very trustworthy, but they do have something you want. Good luck!

Vietnam is now surely hot. Following US diplomatic recognition and a steady increase in travellers last year, a lot of building is going on to accomodate future visitors. But the Vietnam of yesteryear, with its faded French elegance, will probably still be visible for years to come, I hope. Though no scheduled couriers at writing, stay tuned. Many agents in the US will now sell you direct flights to and from Vietnam. Last year I paid about $1300 r/t for Boston-NY-Seoul-Bangkok-Saigon, returning from Hanoi. It's was a lot of money, but I tailored the flight plans to include time in Korea and Thailand for a bit more $$. Expect to pay roughly $300 for a r/t Bangkok-Saigon/Hanoi - many travel agent throughout SE Asia can do it. I'll relish enjoying cold "333" beers on Danang's China Beach at sunset, hitchhiking along old Route One, walking amidst the lunar landscape of the DMZ - leveled by massive carpet bombing, floating in a junk amongst beautiful limestone formations in the Gulf of Tonkin, and everywhere eating incredibly wonderful bowls of the ubiquitous pho, Vietnam's national soup filled with seafood, noodles, getly spiced broth, and bean threads. Now's the time - Go!
