SYBASE, INC.
EMPLOYEE STOCK PURCHASE PLAN OVERVIEW

What is ESPP?

The Employee Stock Purchase Plan affords you an excellent opportunity to invest in Sybase and acquire an equity interest in the company through the purchase of Sybase common stock for at least a 15% discount below the fair market value on the Purchase Date. You can contribute up to 10% of your total compensation toward the purchase of stock through the Plan.

What makes the Sybase ESPP so attractive?

Your original enrollment date price is locked in for a 24-month period which may provide a favorable price for an increased period of time. This is a key feature of the Sybase ESPP as it exceeds industry standards and makes this Plan one of Sybase's most attractive benefits. An example of this feature is illustrated below.

Who is eligible?

You are eligible to participate in this Plan if you are a regular or temporary employee working at least 20 hours per week and five consecutive months in the calendar year. You may enroll in ESPP on the first enrollment date that falls on or after your date of hire.

When can you enroll?

There are two enrollment dates each year. Open enrollment periods are typically held from mid January to mid February for a March 1 enrollment date, and from mid July to mid August for a September 1 enrollment date. During open enrollment you can enroll in the Plan and sign up for direct deposit of stock with Merrill Lynch or Smith Barney/Chase Group.

How to enroll?

An announcement will be made during the open enrollment period. Detailed procedures on how to enroll will be communicated at that time. You will be required to complete and submit an enrollment form to the Human Resources Department by the designated open enrollment deadline.

24-month enrollment feature

The following example illustrates how the ESPP 24-month enrollment feature can work to your advantage. There are four six-month Purchase Periods in a 24-month Enrollment Period. Each Purchase Date will fall on the last business trading day of February and August. Let's say that the closing fair market value of the stock on the original enrollment date is $25.00. The discounted price is $21.25 (85% of $25.00).

However, you continue to purchase stock at 85% of the fair market value on your enrollment date. In fact, the value of your discount has increased over time. On the fourth Purchase Date, your discount has increased to 53%!

What happens if the stock price drops?

In the event that the stock price drops on a subsequent purchase date, the Sybase ESPP is still set up to work to your advantage. The purchase price is always determined as 85% of the LOWER of...

(a) the closing fair market value of Sybase common stock on your Enrollment Date;

...OR...

(b) the closing fair market value of Sybase common stock on each Purchase Date.
