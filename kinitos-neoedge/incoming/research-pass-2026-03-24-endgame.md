# Research Pass - March 24, 2026 - Endgame, Investors, MostFun, Blue Noodle

Purpose: extend the first research pass with a focused look at the later NeoEdge story, including investor history, MostFun, the Blue Noodle phase, and the Double Fusion asset acquisition.

## Key findings

### 1. MostFun was a real consumer-facing experiment, not just a side note

- `src-neoedge-2007-gamespot-bushnell-joins-board`
  - shows MostFun.com in beta in May 2007
  - describes it as free, unlimited, ad-supported games

- `src-mostfun-2007-gamezebo-try-before-you-buy`
  - gives a fuller product description
  - says MostFun offered approximately 130 titles and used video ads in exchange for full-game access

### 2. NeoEdge's investor trail is visible by 2006, but mostly through later funding databases and 2009-2010 press coverage

Stronger press-confirmed financing:

- `src-neoedge-2009-gamesbeat-raises-4m`
  - September 29, 2009
  - says Vanedge Capital co-led a $4M round with Jefferson Partners

- `src-neoedge-2010-gamesbeat-raises-3m`
  - December 21, 2010
  - says MMV Financial provided $3M
  - says NeoEdge had previously raised money from Vanedge Capital and Jefferson Partners

Secondary funding-database evidence worth keeping but not treating as fully canonical yet:

- `src-blue-noodle-2006-gaebler-funding-round`
  - June 30, 2006
  - reports $5.485M from Jefferson Corner Group and VIMAC Ventures

- `src-blue-noodle-2009-gaebler-funding-round`
  - September 29, 2009
  - reports $4M and ties it to revenue growth plus acquisition of premium online casual titles

- `src-neoedge-2026-crunchbase-funding-profile`
  - summarizes four rounds and names Jefferson Partners, VIMAC Ventures, Vanedge Capital, and MMV Capital Partners

Current conclusion:

- no separate Kinitos-only financing source has surfaced yet
- the clearest external financing trail begins after the game/ad-network refocus under NeoEdge / Blue Noodle

### 3. Blue Noodle appears to have been both a rebrand and a social-games push

- `src-blue-noodle-2011-adtechdaily-clickstrip-gdc`
  - says Blue Noodle was "formerly NeoEdge"
  - presents Clickstrip at GDC 2011
  - says Blue Noodle had partnerships with Yahoo! Games, iWin, and RedAtoms

- `src-blue-noodle-2010-uspto-trademark-filing`
  - official USPTO gazette entry for BLUE NOODLE INC.
  - filed March 19, 2010
  - covers downloadable and online games plus related entertainment services

- `src-blue-noodle-2011-fortune-investor-immorality`
  - says Blue Noodle had also been known as NeoEdge Networks and GWN
  - says the company pivoted in 2009 toward Facebook-platform games

### 4. The end of NeoEdge / Blue Noodle is visible as a lender-driven collapse followed by an asset sale

- `src-blue-noodle-2011-fortune-mmv-financial-trouble`
  - shows MMV Financial itself was under stress in July 2011

- `src-blue-noodle-2011-fortune-investor-immorality`
  - says Blue Noodle defaulted on debt
  - says MMV took control of the assets and bank accounts for sale
  - reports that the board resigned and employees were left in limbo
  - says there was already a deal in principle to sell Blue Noodle assets to Double Fusion

- `src-neoedge-2011-gamedeveloper-double-fusion-acquisition`
  - public trade-press confirmation of the acquisition

- `src-double-fusion-2011-adtechdaily-major-expansion-neoedge-assets`
  - explicitly frames the deal as an acquisition of NeoEdge's assets
  - says those assets added more than 100 million monthly video impressions and Canadian distribution

### 5. Patent assignment records provide the clearest direct IP-transfer evidence

- `src-neoedge-ip-2008-google-patents-interstitial-advertising-chain`
  - official Google Patents record for a NeoEdge filing with Steven Woods and Kelly Slough listed as inventors
  - shows assignment to NeoEdge, then security interests involving VIMAC and MMV, then Blue Noodle name-change handling, and finally assignment to Double Fusion in 2011

- `src-neoedge-ip-2010-google-patents-distraction-free-content-chain`
  - official Google Patents record for a NeoEdge / Blue Noodle Flash-game advertising patent family
  - shows the same core chain from NeoEdge to MMV to Blue Noodle and then to Double Fusion in 2011

Current conclusion:

- the archive can now say more confidently that at least some NeoEdge intellectual-property assets were transferred into Double Fusion, not just the publisher network and ad inventory
- what remains unclear is how extensively Double Fusion used or commercialized those specific assets afterward

### 6. Longer-term public evidence of NeoEdge IP use inside Double Fusion is still thin

What I found:

- immediate post-acquisition messaging in December 2011 about integrating NeoEdge's casual/social inventory and sales footprint
- acquisition databases showing the deal completed and the acquired organization was "combined"

What I did not find yet:

- clear later public references to NeoEdge-branded products inside Double Fusion
- obvious public references to specific NeoEdge patents being operated by Double Fusion
- strong post-2011 evidence that MostFun or Blue Noodle titles continued under Double Fusion branding

Current working assessment:

- the best-supported public claim is that Double Fusion acquired and integrated NeoEdge's casual/social ad inventory, related formats, and distribution footprint
- a stronger "ultimate IP use" statement still needs better evidence
