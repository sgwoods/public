# Research Pass - March 24, 2026 - Archived Site Captures

Purpose: capture official or near-official archived web pages that show how NeoEdge, MostFun, and Blue Noodle described themselves directly on their own sites.

## Key findings

### 1. MostFun was explicitly a NeoEdge-operated consumer game network

- `src-mostfun-2007-wayback-homepage-game-player`
  - archived MostFun homepage says users can download the MostFun Game Player and play top puzzle, word, and strategy games with "absolutely no limits"
  - page routes downloads through `ub.neoedge.com`
  - footer includes a visible "Powered by NeoEdge" badge

- `src-mostfun-2008-wayback-homepage-game-network`
  - archived MostFun homepage uses the title "MostFun.com Game Network - Free Unlimited Premium Games"
  - page advertises "100+ free, premium games"
  - internal links include `DetailsNeoARM.aspx`, tying the consumer catalog to NeoEdge's named platform technology
  - footer says "Copyright 2007 NeoEdge Networks"

Current conclusion:

- MostFun was not just a marketing slogan or beta experiment
- it was a branded NeoEdge-operated distribution surface with its own game player, catalog, and technology references

### 2. NeoEdge's own site described patent-pending delivery and ad-insertion technology

- `src-neoedge-2007-wayback-homepage-patent-pending-delivery`
  - archived NeoEdge homepage says its "patent-pending delivery and advertising technology" reduced download cost and gave publishers control plus multiple revenue models
  - also describes "unique dynamic ad insertion technology" and ad placements including in-game, around-game, pre-roll, and post-roll

- `src-neoedge-2008-wayback-homepage-marketplace`
  - archived NeoEdge homepage presents a three-sided model across advertisers, partner websites, and game publishers
  - page links a "Game of the Week" directly into a MostFun `DetailsNeoARM.aspx` page
  - this is strong evidence that the company site and the consumer game network were tightly integrated

Current conclusion:

- the archived NeoEdge site makes the business model much clearer than trade coverage alone
- by 2008 NeoEdge was positioning itself as both an adtech platform and a distribution/monetization layer around downloadable casual games

### 3. Blue Noodle's site shows the later rebrand and social-games framing directly

- `src-blue-noodle-2011-wayback-homepage-casual-social`
  - archived homepage title is "BlueNoodle | The Leading Ad Platform For Casual Games"
  - meta description says Blue Noodle is the leader in casual and social game advertising
  - homepage splits the audience into advertisers and developers
  - page says developers can monetize "100% of your players" with high-performance video ad solutions
  - news banner highlights the MMV Financial financing announcement

Current conclusion:

- Blue Noodle was not just a legal rename
- the site presents a more polished casual/social ad-platform identity while still matching the NeoEdge monetization story
